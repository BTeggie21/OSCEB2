🌸 GENERAL PRELIMINARIES (All Stations)

📝 Detailed Steps
  • Introduce: "Good Morning Sir/Ma’am, I am [Name], a first year medical student from Bicol University."
  • Language preference: "Are you comfortable with me speaking in English or do you have any preferred language? (Bikol, Filipino,) Need a translator?"
  • Confirm ID: "May I know your name and date of birth? How do you want to be addressed?"
  • Explain and ask for consent: "I will be asking a series of questions/examining your [body part]. Any information that you will share will be kept confidential. Can we start?"
  • Perform hand hygiene before contact.
  • Vital signs: "But first, I'll be taking your vital signs."

🌸 ABDOMEN (Order: I → Am → Pe → Pa)

💡 NOTE: “Is there any area of your abdomen that is painful? Can you point it?” Position: Supine (pillow under the neck and knees). Stand on right side of the bed.
📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Inspection: Assess skin (color, striae, scar, temp, dilated veins, rashes), umbilicus (inflammation, contour, bulges), contour (flat, flabby, round, protuberant, globular), visible peristalsis, and palpation at epigastrium.
  • Auscultation: Done first so as not to alter bowel sound. Assess bowel motility using diaphragm. Check frequency and character (5-34/min clicks and gurgles). Listen for borborygmi, bruits (aorta, iliac artery, femoral), friction rubs (liver and spleen). If with HTN: check epigastrium and upper quadrants.
  • Percussion: Assess for gas across 4 quadrants. Right: liver dullness; left: tympanitic air and splenic flexure of colon. Check for shifting dullness and fluid wave.
  • Palpation (Light): Gentle dipping motion. Ask patient to exhale, relax, mouth open.
  • Palpation (Deep): Assess liver edge, kidneys, and abdominal mass.
  • Liver Exam: Percuss at right midclavicular line (level below umbilicus upward, nipple line downward until dull). Palpate while patient takes a deep breath. Use hooking technique for obese patients.
  • Spleen Exam: Percuss left lower anterior chest wall (6th rib to ant/mid axillary). Normal: Traube space not obliterated → tympanitic. Check splenic percussion sign (patient takes deep breath → percuss again → tympanitic). Palpate: reach around to support left rib cage, press in toward spleen below left costal margin, ask for deep breath. Repeat with patient lying on right side (flex leg, deep breath).
  • Kidney Exam: Palpation below 12th rib. Left: go to patient's left side (rarely palpable). Right: go to right side (palpable in thin people). Percussion: if tender, use kidney punch test at CVA (12th rib). Place non-dominant hand, produce firm thud. Repeat other side.
  • Ascites: Shifting dullness (percuss border of tympany/dullness supine → roll to side → percuss again. Normal: border constant). Fluid wave.
  • Appendicitis: Ask where pain began & where it is now. Ask to cough to see where pain occurs. Palpate carefully for local tenderness.

🌸 THORAX & CHEST

📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Posterior Chest (Position: Sitting | Order: I-Pa-Pe-A):
    • Inspection: Stand midline behind. Assess symmetry (shape & movement), retraction, respiratory movement (quiet, unlabored).
    • Palpation: Assess skin abnormalities (masses, sinus tracts), crepitus (normally none), lung expansion (symmetrical at 10th rib, arms folded, deep breath), tactile fremitus (symmetrical, say "99", ladder approach: 1 tracheal, 4 vesicular).
    • Percussion: Arms on chest. Chest resonant. Diaphragmatic excursion: deep breath & hold, percuss boundary dullness/resonance. Dullness on full inspiration 3-5.5 cm.
    • Auscultation: Ask to cough to clear mucus. Use diaphragm, ladder pattern (one full breath/location). Breathe deeply with mouth open. Vocal resonance ("99"). Normal: soft/low pitched, rustling on inspiration, softer on expiration. I:E ratio 3:1. No wheezing/stridor/crackles.
  • Anterior Chest (Position: Supine, slightly abducted arms):
    • Inspection: Symmetry, deformity, muscle retraction, respiratory movement (quiet, unlabored, no accessory muscles). Check for cyanosis, pursed lips, nasal flaring, clubbing (Schamroth window test: normal = diamond present).
    • Palpation: Tenderness, bruising, skin changes, chest expansion, tactile fremitus.
    • Percussion: Ladder pattern.
    • Auscultation: Same ladder pattern, deep breath open mouth.
  • Breast Female (Timing: 5-7 days post-menses | Order: I-Pa):
    • Inspection (Sitting): Remove shirt. Assess color, thickening, symmetry, contour, nipple. Raise arms over head, hands on hips, lean forward → check for dimpling/retraction.
    • Palpation (Supine): 3 mins each breast. Small concentric circles, light/medium/deep pressure at each point. Use pads of 2nd-4th fingers. Lateral: roll opposite hip, hand on forehead. Medial: shoulders flat, hand at neck, lift elbow even with shoulder.
  • Breast Male: Inspection with palpation (sit). Inspect nipple/areola (no discharge). Palpate areola & tissue for nodules.
  • Axilla: Inspection: skin of each axilla. Palpation: arm down & relax. Palpate left axilla with right hand (and vice versa).

🌸 NEUROLOGICAL EXAMINATION

📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Mental Status: Assess alertness, appropriateness, orientation to PPT. Cognitive: remember 3 words ("apple, penny, table"), language (conversation, commands, reading, writing), arithmetic (3+7), R-L orientation, drawing (circle), gnostic functions (name objects), memory (remote, recent, immediate). Mini-Mental: Orientation to time, registration, naming, reading ("CLOSE YOUR EYES").
  • CN I (Olfactory): Present familiar non-irritating odors. Close eyes, compress one side, sniff, identify smell.
  • CN II (Optic): Visual acuity (Snellen/Jaeger), visual fields (both then separate, subtle movements, all quadrants). Ophthalmoscopy: clear media, distinct disc margin, normal AV ratio, no hemorrhages.
  • CN III, IV, VI: EOMs, pupils, nystagmus. Follow finger H-pattern, convergence, focus. Pupillary reaction (swinging flashlight test direct/consensual).
  • CN V (Trigeminal): Corneal reflex (look up/away, cotton wisp, avoid lashes). Sensory (tap ophthalmic/maxillary/mandibular). Motor (touch masseter/temporal, clench jaw, pterygoids open/close/lateral).
  • CN VII (Facial): Motor: raise eyebrows, close eyes tight, show teeth. Sensory: taste anterior 2/3 tongue (sugar solution cotton bud).
  • CN VIII (Vestibulocochlear): Hearing (rub hair/fingers). Weber test (buzzing sound, which side louder?). Rinne test (BC vs AC, normal 2:1). Romberg test.
  • CN IX, X: Gag, swallowing, uvula, nasal twang, hoarseness. Say "ah" or yawn. Soft palate rises symmetrically, uvula midline.
  • CN XI (Accessory): Trapezius (raise shoulders), SCM (turn head against resistance; test opposite side).
  • CN XII (Hypoglossal): Speech, tongue deviation. Protrude tongue (midline). Say "lalalalala", "mimimi", "kakakaka". Check for slurring.
  • Motor Function: Strength: Upper (proximal: arms outstretched, distal: grasp). Lower (proximal: walk, rise from chair; distal: tiptoe). Pronator drift: arms extended parallel, eyes closed, maintain ~10s. Grade 0-5 (Normal: 5). Tone: resistance to passive movement (upper/lower limbs), check jerkiness/increased/decreased resistance.
  • Sensory Function: Crude: touch, pain (pinprick), temp, vibration (tuning fork at bony prominences), joint position sense (close eyes, move finger up/down). Romberg. Cortical: graphesthesia, stereognosis, bilateral simultaneous stimulation, point localization.
  • Reflexes: DTRs: Biceps (C5-6, observe flexion), Brachioradialis (C6-7, strike 1-2 inches above wrist), Triceps (C6-7, extension), Patellar (L2-4), Ankle Jerk (S1-2, strike Achilles). Plantar: stroke lateral sole to ball. Abdominal: stroke above (T8-10) & below (T10-12) umbilicus → umbilicus moves toward stimulus. Pathologic: check clonus, Hoffman.

🌸 CARDIAC EXAM

📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Inspection: Chest deformities and scars, check for PMI, chest symmetry & shape.
  • Jugular Vein Pressure: Identify pulsation at highest point of neck (start at 30°). Study waves. Measure JVP (distinguish from carotid, observe undulation). Normal: 6-8 cm H2O. Hepatojugular reflex: apply pressure on liver for 10s, observe rise (>3cm after 15s abnormal).
  • Palpation: Palpate 2nd right/left interspace, along sternal border, apex. Palpate PMI at 5th ICS, 7-9 cm lateral to midsternal line. Observe diameter, amplitude, duration.
  • Auscultation: Listen for S1/S2 at 4 areas ("All Pray To Mama"): 1. Aortic (2nd ICS, right parasternal), 2. Pulmonary (2nd ICS, left parasternal), 3. Tricuspid (4th ICS, parasternal), 4. Mitral (5th ICS, midclavicular).
  • Peripheral Components:
    • Skin: Peripheral (lift hands supine, fingers together, pronate, check temp, cap refill, ears). Central (conjunctiva, open mouth check mucosa/cheeks/tongue, Schamroth's test).
    • Pulses: Examine amplitude, rate, rhythm, symmetry. Radial (10s), both wrists. Brachial, carotid (one at a time). Normal: 2+ (brisk, expected).

No extra heart sounds or murmurs are heard. Peripheral pulses are 2+ and symmetric. Capillary refill is under 2 seconds. No clubbing, cyanosis, or edema is noted — normal cardiac exam."

🌸 HEAD AND NECK PE

📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Note signs of syndromic facies, large tumors, skin lesions.
  • Hair: Loss, distribution, number (pull test, tug).
  • Scalp: Lesions, indentation, scars.
  • Face: Color, drooling (Bell's/stroke), expression (close/open eyes, smile, frown), test numbness.
  • Eyes: Pupillary response, EOM, light touch sensation, sclera/conjunctival color, eyelid lesions, iris dilation/constriction, nystagmus. Test VA, visual fields, direct/consensual reflex, H-pattern, convergence.
  • Ears: Hearing test (hair/fingers → Rinne/Weber). Lesion, tenderness, palpate mastoid. TM via otoscope.
  • Nose: Midline, deviated septum, discharge, clogged, check inside for redness. Examine with nasal speculum.
  • Oral Cavity: Examine (CN IX/X), tooth decay, palpate base of tongue/floor of mouth/tonsils.
  • Neck: Turn head against resistance. Palpate lymph nodes (1-4). Note any nodes. Palpate thyroid nodules (moves on swallowing vs lymph nodes stay).

🌸 SKIN PE

📝 Detailed PE Steps
  • Prelims: Request patient to change into gown with opening in back (clothes removed except underwear). Ask if chaperone needed. Hand hygiene, VS.
  • Inspection & Palpation (Systematic Head-to-Toe):
    • Skin lesions: Number, size, color, shape, texture, primary lesion type (flat/raised), location, configuration, melanoma screening.
    • Regions: Head & neck → Hair & scalp → Shoulder, arms, hands → Chest & abdomen → Thighs & lower legs, feet, toes → Lower back, posterior legs.
    • Test for hair loss.
✅ Normal Findings & Reporting Summary
ComponentNormal Finding
GeneralSkin warm and dry
NailsWithout clubbing or cyanosis; normal capillary refill
Lesions/PigmentationApprox. 20 brown, round macules on upper back, chest, arms; symmetric pigmentation; none suspicious for malignancy
OtherNo rash, petechiae, or ecchymoses. Normal hair distribution/texture.
🗣️ OSCE Verbalization Scripts
"On examination from head to toes, the skin is warm and dry with uniform coloration. Nails show no clubbing or cyanosis, and capillary refill is less than 2 seconds. Approximately 20 brown, round macules are noted on the upper back, chest, and arms, all symmetric in pigmentation with none suspicious for malignancy. No rashes, petechiae, or ecchymoses are observed. Hair distribution and texture are normal. Overall skin examination is within normal limits."
💡 Pro Tip for OSCE Verbalization: Always use the formula:
"On [STEP], I observe/feel/hear [FINDING], which is [NORMAL/EXPECTED]."
State the step first, describe exactly what you assess, and conclude with the normal interpretation. Keep it under 2 sentences per finding. This demonstrates active observation and clinical reasoning.
