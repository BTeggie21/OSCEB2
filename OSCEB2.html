🗣️ OSCE Verbalization Scripts
✅ Inspection: "On inspection, the abdomen is flat, symmetric, with no visible scars, striae, dilated veins, or peristalsis. The umbilicus is inverted and without inflammation — all normal findings." ✅ Auscultation: "On auscultation using the diaphragm, I hear normoactive bowel sounds, approximately 10-15 clicks and gurgles per minute in all quadrants. No bruits are heard over the aorta, iliac, or femoral areas — normal." ✅ Percussion: "On percussion, all four quadrants are tympanitic. Liver span is approximately 6 cm at the right midclavicular line. There is no shifting dullness, no fluid wave, and no CVA tenderness — normal." ✅ Palpation: "On light palpation, the abdomen is soft with no superficial masses or tenderness. On deep palpation, no deep masses, and the liver, spleen, and kidneys are not palpable — all within normal limits."
📊 PDF Reporting Summary (Verbatim)
● Inspection: Skin is brown, no lesions, no scars; it is symmetric, no localized bulging, no swelling; shape is flat & umbilicus is inverted; no visible peristalsis, no superficial dilated veins, no abnormal pulsations ● Auscultation: Normoactive bowel sounds, no bruit heard over the epigastrium, right & left paraumbilical areas ● Percussion: The 4 quadrants are tympanitic; the liver span is 6cm & no splenic dullness; no fluid wave, no shifting dullness; no CVA tenderness ● Palpation: On light palpation, the abdomen is soft, no palpable superficial mass & tenderness; On deep palpation, no deep mass & tenderness. The liver, spleen, right & left kidneys are not palpable.

🗣️ OSCE Verbalization Scripts
✅ Posterior Chest Inspection: "On inspection, the posterior chest is symmetric in shape and movement, with no retractions. Respirations are quiet and unlabored — normal." ✅ Palpation: "On palpation, there is no crepitus or tenderness. Chest expansion is symmetric at the level of the 10th rib. Tactile fremitus is equal bilaterally when the patient says '99' — normal." ✅ Percussion: "On percussion, the lung fields are resonant throughout. Diaphragmatic excursion is approximately 4 cm bilaterally — normal." ✅ Auscultation: "On auscultation, breath sounds are vesicular with an I:E ratio of 3:1. No adventitious sounds such as wheezes or crackles are heard. Vocal resonance is normal — all findings within normal limits."
📊 PDF Reporting Summary (Verbatim)
● Inspection: The chest is brown, no lesions, no visible dilated vessels, with normal muscle development; Thorax is symmetric, elliptical, no deformity; With normal rate of respiration, effortless breathing, with inspiratory phase longer than expiration, & with symmetric lung expansion ● Palpation: No tenderness, no palpable mass; Symmetrical chest expansion both in upper & lower lung field; Normal & equal tactile fremitus on both sides anteriorly, laterally & posteriorly ● Percussion: Normal lung percussion note: The entire lung area is resonant ● Auscultation: Breath sounds are vesicular on the entire lung area except beneath manubrium sterni & upper interscapular region (bronchovesicular); Negative for adventitious sound; Negative bronchophony, egophony, and whispered pectoriloquy

🧠 QUICK MNEMONICS FOR NORMAL FINDINGS

ABDOMEN: "I Am Pretty Patient" → Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation
CHEST: "Inspect, Palpate, Percuss, Auscultate" → Always auscultate lungs BEFORE palpating abdomen!
PUPILS: "EBRTL" → Equal, Briskly Reactive To Light
REFLEXES: "2+ is Just Right" → Grade 2+ = normal DTR
CARDIAC: "All Pray To Mama" → Aortic, Pulmonic, Tricuspid, Mitral valve areas
NEURO: "CN I-XII Normal = Function Intact" → Test each, document symmetry & appropriateness
💡 Pro Tip for OSCE: Always verbalize normal findings! Use the formula:
"On [STEP], I observe [FINDING], which is [NORMAL/EXPECTED]."
This shows the examiner you know what to look for—even when it's normal. Keep it concise (under 2 sentences per finding), state the step first, describe what you see/feel/hear, and add the normal interpretation.
